I am a computational epidemiologist and AI researcher based in Tāmaki Makaurau Auckland,
Aotearoa New Zealand. My PhD is in computational cosmology, but I have spent the last several
years building models at the intersection of infectious disease, health data science, and
responsible AI — with Python as the primary tool for everything from ETL pipelines to
ensemble forecasting.

Lately, I have been increasingly interested in *how* AI tools get deployed in health systems
— and specifically, whether the people who need them most are actually well-served by them.
That question is what drove me to build the equity evaluation framework I am presenting
at this conference.

I care a lot about open, reproducible research: the code for this talk is MIT-licensed
and designed to be adapted. If you work in health, data science, or responsible AI —
or you are just curious why aggregate accuracy metrics can be quietly dangerous —

I hope this talk is useful to you.
